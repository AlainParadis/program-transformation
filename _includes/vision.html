Future Program Vision

Algonquin's existing diploma programs in graphic design prepare students for jobs that are flattening or being absorbed by generative AI. The Ontario job outlook for NOC 52120 is rated limited for 2024 to 2026. But the demand for strategic, human-centred design above the layer AI automates is accelerating. A three-year applied degree in design studies positions Algonquin to lead this shift in Ontario. It captures degree-seeking students who currently choose universities. It carries a higher tuition envelope. It directly executes three of Algonquin’s 2025-2030 strategic priorities.

“The future of design is human-led, AI-enhanced.”
Why this program? Why now?

Routine production work is being absorbed by generative AI tools. Strategic, conceptual, and human-centred design is growing in value. A graduate prepared only for execution will struggle. A graduate prepared to direct AI, frame ambiguous problems, and own strategic decisions will thrive.

Most Ontario polytechnics now offer or are launching design degrees. Conestoga has a Bachelor of Design (Honours). Humber offers a Bachelor of Design in User Experience Design. Algonquin’s current ceiling at the advanced diploma puts us behind. Students who want degree recognition are leaving the region.

Students increasingly seek the college pedagogy advantage of smaller class sizes, hands-on learning, and industry partnerships, paired with a degree-level credential. A three-year applied bachelor’s is the credential built for this exact preference.

The 2025-2030 Strategic Plan names “Career-focused, lifelong learning,” “Technology and intelligence,” and “Partnering for prosperity” as our three priorities. This program operationalises all three in one credential.
