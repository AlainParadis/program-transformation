Future Program Vision

Algonquin's existing diploma program in graphic design prepare students for jobs that are flattening or being absorbed by generative AI. The Ontario job outlook for NOC 52120.01 (Graphic Design) is rated limited for 2024 to 2026. But the demand for strategic, human-centred design above the layer AI automates is accelerating. A three-year applied degree in design studies positions Algonquin to lead this shift in Ontario. It captures degree-seeking students who currently choose universities. It carries a higher tuition envelope. It directly executes three of Algonquin’s 2025-2030 strategic priorities. Read more about the broad appeal of this proposed program.

“The future of design is human-led, AI-enhanced.”

A proposed {{ site.data.sitewide.program-title }} implies a degree‑level complexity in knowledge, analysis, and independent work. It will have a volume of breadth and depth that exceeds an advanced diploma but is still less than a typical four‑year honours program.

A degree credential increases program differentiation, supports recruitment in a competitive landscape, and positions Algonquin as a leader in AI-integrated applied design education. A degree program will attract students who might otherwise choose university pathways.

What Graduates Will Do

Graduates will be prepared for occupations classified under four National Occupational Classification codes: NOC 21233 (Web Designers), NOC 11202 (Professional Occupations in Advertising, Marketing and Public Relations), NOC NOC 52120 (Graphic Designers), and NOC 52113 (Other Creative Designers). NOC 21233 and 11202 require a bachelor's degree as the typical credential and represent the primary occupational pathway opened by this program. NOC 52120 represents the traditional disciplinary anchor and continues to be served by the program, with the degree credential positioning graduates for senior and strategic roles within the occupation.

Why this program? Why now?

The field of design is a fulfilling, profitable career notwithstanding the perceived challenges posed by automation and artificial intelligence. Routine production work is being absorbed by generative AI tools. Strategic, conceptual, and human-centred design is growing in value. A graduate prepared only for execution will struggle. A graduate prepared to direct AI, frame ambiguous problems, and own strategic decisions will thrive.

Many Ontario polytechnics now offer or are launching design degrees. Conestoga has a Bachelor of Design (Honours). Humber offers a Bachelor of Design in User Experience Design. Algonquin’s current ceiling at the advanced diploma puts us behind. Students who want degree recognition are leaving the region.

"A.I. is not replacing creativity. It is amplifying it."

Students appreciate the college pedagogy advantage of smaller class sizes, hands-on learning, and industry partnerships, paired with a degree-level credential. A three-year applied bachelor’s is the credential built for this exact preference.

The 2025-2030 Strategic Plan names “Career-focused, lifelong learning,” “Technology and intelligence,” and “Partnering for prosperity” as our three priorities. This program operationalises all three in one credential.
