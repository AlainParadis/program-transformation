Future Program Vision

The demand for strategic, human-centred design above the layer AI automates is accelerating. A three-year applied degree in design studies positions Algonquin to lead this shift in Ontario. It captures degree-seeking students who currently choose universities. It carries a higher tuition envelope. It directly executes three of Algonquin’s 2025-2030 strategic priorities. Read more about the broad appeal of this proposed program.

“The future of design is human-led, AI-enhanced.”

A proposed {{ site.data.sitewide.program-title }} implies a degree‑level complexity in knowledge, analysis, and independent work. Senior design practice calls for design research, evidence-based argumentation, systems thinking, professional autonomy, and a clear sense of the limits of one’s own knowledge. These are degree-level competencies, not production skills. They are also the parts of design practice that emerging technologies do not automate. As routine production is absorbed by generative tools, the strategic and human-centred work that remains is precisely the work the Baccalaureate Degree Level Standard describes.

The labour market already recognizes this level. Senior, strategic, and leadership design roles increasingly use a bachelor’s degree as the typical hiring credential. Statistics Canada earnings data shows a sustained premium for degree holders that reflects it.

A degree credential increases program differentiation, supports recruitment in a competitive landscape, and positions Algonquin as a leader in AI-integrated applied design education. A degree program will attract students who might otherwise choose university pathways.

The college already delivers design programming. Much of the infrastructure a degree requires is therefore in place. Design studios, computer labs, current industry software, and library and electronic resources support existing programs. This established base means the college has the capacity to deliver the program. It is not starting from zero on physical and technical capacity. The Development phase will confirm possible additional studio capacity, specialized equipment, and resource upgrades the degree level may require.

What Graduates Will Do

Graduates will be prepared for occupations classified under four National Occupational Classification codes: NOC 21233 (Web Designers), NOC 11202 (Professional Occupations in Advertising, Marketing and Public Relations), NOC NOC 52120 (Graphic Designers), and NOC 52113 (Other Creative Designers). NOC 21233 and 11202 require a bachelor's degree as the typical credential and represent the primary occupational pathway opened by this program. NOC 52120 represents the traditional disciplinary anchor and continues to be served by the program, with the degree credential positioning graduates for senior and strategic roles within the occupation.

Why this program? Why now?

The need here is a skills gap, not a shortage of designers. There are plenty of designers. There are too few trained for where the work is going. Routine production is being absorbed by generative tools. The value is migrating to strategic, human-centred, and AI-fluent practice. That is the work this program trains for. The gap is between the skills the labour force has and the skills the market increasingly wants.

The labour market data reflects exactly this split. The traditional production segment shows oversupply. New job seekers outnumber new openings, and growth is slow. That sounds like a problem until you see what it means. The surplus is in the shrinking core. The program is not built for the core. It is built for the growth pockets. User experience is the named growth segment within the field. Demand in the strategic and marketing-adjacent roles is reported as strong. Web and interactive roles show balanced outlooks. The oversupply signal and the program thesis are the same story told from two ends. The field has too many people trained for the work that is fading. It has too few trained for the work that is rising.

Student demand is the open question, and the honest answer is that it needs testing. The signals are encouraging but not yet proof. The college already has an applicant pipeline and a laddering pathway feeding its design programs. High-school applicants increasingly weigh degree recognition when they choose. And students in this region who want a design degree currently leave to get one, which is unmet demand by another name. The applicant-demand study, drawing on OCAS data, will test this directly during the next phase. That study is the proof point. These signals are the reason to commission it.

Industry is already engaged. The Program Advisory Committee has raised the shift toward strategic and AI-integrated practice directly. Industry partners are built into the program through hackathons, collaborative studios, and the capstone immersion. Letters of support from employers will formalize this as the program develops.

Many Ontario polytechnics now offer or are launching design degrees. Conestoga, in Kitchener, Ontario has a Bachelor of Design (Honours). Humber, in Toronto, offers a Bachelor of Design in User Experience Design. A student in Ottawa who wants this credential has to leave the region to get it.

That is not a small inconvenience. It removes them from their home, their support network, and often the local employers they would otherwise build relationships with. Eastern Ontario has the population and the design employers to support a program of this kind. The region’s students currently face a choice between leaving or settling for a different credential. This program closes that gap.

"A.I. is not replacing creativity. It is amplifying it."

Students appreciate the college pedagogy advantage of smaller class sizes, hands-on learning, and industry partnerships, paired with a degree-level credential. A three-year applied bachelor’s is the credential built for this exact preference.

The 2025-2030 Strategic Plan names “Career-focused, lifelong learning,” “Technology and intelligence,” and “Partnering for prosperity” as our three priorities. This program operationalises all three in one credential.
