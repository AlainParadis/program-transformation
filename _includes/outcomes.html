Graduate Outcomes

The Bachelor of Applied Design (Design Studies) aims to prepare graduates for a design industry shaped by emerging technologies and shifting professional demands. The outcomes below reflect the degree-level expectations of the Ontario Qualifications Framework, spanning depth of knowledge, research competency, strategic thinking, and professional accountability. The degree level does not result in an unwarranted raising of the credential in the field. The Baccalaureate learning outcomes are warranted by the nature of the work the program prepares graduates to do.

The PEQAB Standard requires the proposed program meets the Bachelor’s Degree Level Standard on the Ontario Qualifications Framework across the six following elements which are mapped to the program learning outcomes below.

Taken together, the outcomes describe the kind of thinking a bachelor’s graduate is expected to do. Graduates work across design disciplines. They go deep in a chosen area. They run their own research, weigh competing approaches on the evidence, and build arguments they can defend with scholarly sources. They communicate those decisions clearly to specialists and non-specialists alike. They take responsibility for their independent and collaborative work, hold to professional and ethical standards, and keep learning as the field shifts. They understand the limits of their own knowledge, accounting for uncertainty rather than papering over it. That last quality, more than any single skill, is what marks degree-level practice apart from technical competence.
