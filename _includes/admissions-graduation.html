Admission, Promotion and Graduation

Admission, promotion, and graduation requirements follow the Ontario Qualifications Framework and Algonquin’s existing degree policies. The program adds nothing exotic. It sets the standard degree baseline. It ties entry to the outcomes the program teaches.

  1. Entry. Direct entry requires an Ontario Secondary School Diploma or equivalent. A Grade 12 English credit is required. The program is communication-intensive. The English prerequisite ties admission to the communication outcomes at degree level. Mature applicants enter through Algonquin’s mature-student route. That route verifies equivalent ability by prior postsecondary work or an entrance assessment.
  2. Laddering. Graduates of the graphic design diploma enter through a bridge into an upper year. This is the same pipeline named in the demand case. As an admission pathway it carries a requirement. The Development phase will produce the gap analysis. That analysis compares diploma outcomes to degree outcomes. It addresses the difficulty gap, the content and skills gap, and the breadth gap. It also estimates how many graduates complete the degree by laddering in.
  3. Prior learning. Credit for prior learning follows Algonquin’s PLAR policy. Credit is awarded for demonstrated learning, not for experience. Prior learning assessment does not exceed 50 percent of program credits. Transfer credit is separate from that cap.
  4. Promotion. The minimum achievement for progression is not lower than C-, consistent with the framework. Academic remediation, sanction, and suspension follow Algonquin’s academic-standing policy.
  5. Graduation. Graduation requires all thirty courses and the 300-hour work term. The work term is a degree requirement, not an add-on.
