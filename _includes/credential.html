Credential

A proposed Bachelor of Applied Design implies a degree‑level complexity in knowledge, analysis, and independent work. It will include a major field with significant upper‑level work, but over three years. It will have a volume of breadth and depth that exceeds an advanced diploma but is still less than a typical four‑year honours program.

Decision Criteria

  • Postsecondary Education Quality Assessment Board (PEQAB) feasibility (faculty credentials, scholarship, breadth).
  • Student demand and labour-market outcomes.
  • Financial sustainability.
  • Alignment with Algonquin’s Program Quality Assurance Framework and strategic plan.
  • Impact on faculty workload, culture, and equity.

Faculty Credentials

The minimum credential for faculty teaching in a 3-year applied degree program is the terminal credential, which is a Master's degree. Fifty percent of core courses are taught by Master's holders. There's a minimum of five years' of work experience. All faculty are required to maintain currency in their field. There are no requirements for professional credentials.
